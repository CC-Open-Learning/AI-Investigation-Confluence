  2. Digital Teaching Assistant(DTA): W25 Overview

    1. Concept :

      DTA is designed to

      • Simulate real-world learning scenarios.

      • Emphasize reinforcement learning and reflection to help students better understand and apply knowledge.

    2. Key features

features

(1)

Present Scenario Summary

Introduces students to the scenario’s context and objectives, setting the stage for a meaningful interaction.

(2)

DTA Pre-Refection(Pre-Coaching)

Students assess their readiness and identify potential challenges, encouraging proactive engagement with the scenario.

  1. Readiness Assessment Questions:

  • “Do you understand what needs to be done in this scenario?”

  • “Have you experienced a similar situation before?”

  • “Do you feel confident in the skills or knowledge required for this scenario?”

  1. Challenge Identification Questions:

  • “What do you think will be the most difficult part of this task?”

  • “What additional preparations do you think you might need to complete this scenario?”

  • “Which part of this scenario do you expect to take the most time?”

  1. Proactive Engagement Questions:

  • “What strategy do you plan to use to successfully complete this scenario?”

  • “What resources or tools would you like to use?”

(3)

Interactive Scenarios

Simulate real-world tasks in dynamic and engaging ways to provide practical learning experiences.

(4)

DTA Post Reflection(Post-Coaching)

DTA delivers personalized feedback and specific recommendations for improvement.

  1. Encouraging Self-Assessment:

The AI prompts students to reflect on their learning performance and evaluate themselves with questions like:

  • “What was the most challenging part of this scenario?”

  • “What could you have done differently to achieve a better outcome?”

  • “Do you feel you’ve met your learning objectives? Why or why not?”

  1. Providing Personalized Feedback:

  • The DTA delivers specific feedback on areas where the student excelled and areas needing improvement.

  • It explains how the student’s self-assessment aligns or differs from their actual performance, enhancing self-awareness.

  1. Suggesting Learning Improvements:

Based on the student’s performance, the DTA provides recommendations for further learning, such as:

  • “If you want to dive deeper into risk management, review Unit 3.2.”

  • “Here are additional practice materials you might find helpful before moving to the next step.”

(5)

AI Summary

The DTA summarizes the student’s learning performance and offers an option to save the summary as a learning record, if needed.  

  • AI-generated evaluations are aligned with a predefined rubric to ensure clear and structured assessment criteria.

  • Students can save these summaries as learning artifacts and optionally submit them for review or grading.
