AI Investigation : Types of Advanced Prompting Techniques

Created by Jongeon on Feb 18, 2025

  • Zero-Shot Prompting: Asking the model a direct question without providing any prior examples.

    • Example: "Explain the benefits of apples."

  • Few-Shot Prompting: Provide a few examples to guide the model in learning a pattern.

    • Example: "Translate English words into French. Example: cat → chat, dog → chien. Now translate 'bird'."

  • Chain of Thought (CoT) Prompting: Encouraging the model to break down complex problems step by step to enhance reasoning.

    • Example: "Five students each have three apples. Explain step by step how to calculate the total number of apples."

  • ReAct (Reasoning + Acting) Prompting: Combining reasoning and action to generate responses, useful for conversational AI and decision-support systems.

    • Example: "You are a travel guide. Plan a one-day itinerary in Paris and provide a brief description of each location."

  • Chain of Verification (CoVe) Prompting: Adding a verification step where the model checks the accuracy of its response.

    • Example: "Explain the circumference of the Earth and verify the source of the information."

  • Chain of Note (CoN) Prompting: Guiding the model to explore a topic in-depth by creating structured notes step by step.

    • Example: "Write key events in the history of artificial intelligence in a structured note format."

  • Prompt Chaining: Breaking down a complex task into multiple sub-tasks processed sequentially.

    • Example: "First, research the latest technology trends, then summarize the findings."

  • Fukatsu-Style Prompt Framework: Structuring model output by providing specific instructions and constraints, ensuring the response follows a particular format.

    • Example: "Provide the following information in JSON format: name, age, occupation."

  • Expert Prompting: Assigning the model a role as an expert in a specific field to generate more specialized responses.

    • Example: "You are a data scientist. Explain the latest trends in machine learning."

  • Generated Knowledge Prompting: Encouraging the model to generate new insights based on existing knowledge.

    • Example: "Predict how technology trends will evolve over the next 10 years based on current developments."

  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG): Enhancing responses by retrieving external data from databases to provide accurate and up-to-date information.

    • Example: "Explain the latest AI research trends." (The model searches for recent academic papers to provide a comprehensive answer.)
